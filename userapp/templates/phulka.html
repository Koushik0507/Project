    • Phulka

    Phulka is a type of Indian flatbread made from whole wheat flour, often served with curries, vegetables, or lentils. Unlike other flatbreads.

    Ingredients

    • 1 cup whole wheat flour (atta)
    • Water for kneading dough
    • Flour for dusting

    Instructions

    1.

    In a mixing bowl, add whole wheat flour.

    2.

    Gradually add water and knead into a smooth dough. The dough should be soft but not sticky.

    3.

    Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.

    4.

    Take one dough ball and roll it into a small circle, about 6-7 inches in diameter, using a rolling pin. Dust with flour as needed to prevent sticking.

    5.

    Heat a tawa (griddle) over medium-high heat.

    6.

    Place the rolled dough circle on the hot tawa and cook for about 20-30 seconds until you see bubbles forming on the surface.

    7.

    Flip the phulka with a pair of tongs and cook the other side for about 30-40 seconds until it starts to puff up.

    8.

    Now, hold the phulka with a pair of tongs and directly place it on the open flame. It will puff up completely within a few seconds.

    9.

    Remove the phulka from the flame and place it in a clean cloth or a covered container to keep it soft and warm.

    10.

    Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.

    11.

    Serve hot phulkas with your favorite curry or vegetable dish. Enjoy your homemade soft and fluffy phulkas!

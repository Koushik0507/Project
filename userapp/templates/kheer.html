    • Kheer

    Kheer is a traditional Indian dessert made by boiling rice or vermicelli in milk, sweetened with sugar, and flavored with cardamom, saffron, or other aromatic spices. Often garnished with nuts like almonds, cashews, or pistachios, Kheer has a creamy texture and a delightful aroma.

    Ingredients

    • 1/2 cup rice
    • 4 cups milk
    • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
    • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • Saffron strands (optional)
    • Chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios) for garnish

    Instructions

    1.

    Wash rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes.

    2.

    Drain the rice and set it aside.

    3.

    In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring milk to a boil over medium heat.

    4.

    Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling milk. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

    5.

    Simmer the rice and milk mixture on low heat, stirring frequently, until the rice is cooked and the mixture thickens. This may take about 30-40 minutes.

    6.

    Once the rice is cooked and the mixture has thickened, add sugar and mix well until it dissolves completely.

    7.

    Add cardamom powder and saffron strands (if using), and mix well.

    8.

    Cook for another 5-10 minutes until the kheer reaches the desired consistency.

    9.

    Remove from heat and let it cool for a while.

    10.

    Garnish with chopped nuts.

    11.

    Serve warm or chilled. Enjoy your creamy and delicious kheer!

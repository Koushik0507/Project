    • Jalebi

    Jalebi is a popular Indian dessert made by deep-frying spiral-shaped batter and soaking it in a sugar syrup. These golden, crispy spirals have a unique, chewy texture and a sweet, tangy flavor.

    Ingredients

    • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
    • 2 tablespoons gram flour (besan)
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder (for color)
    • 1 cup water
    • 1 teaspoon lemon juice or yogurt
    • Oil or ghee for frying
    • For sugar syrup: - 1 cup sugar - 1/2 cup water - A few saffron strands - 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder - 1/2 teaspoon rose water (optional)

    Instructions

    1.

    In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, gram flour, baking powder, and turmeric powder.

    2.

    Gradually add water and lemon juice (or yogurt) to make a smooth batter. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter.

    3.

    Cover the batter and let it ferment for 8-10 hours or overnight.

    4.

    In a saucepan, prepare the sugar syrup by combining sugar, water, saffron strands, and cardamom powder. Bring to a boil and simmer for 5-7 minutes until the syrup thickens slightly. Add rose water if using, and then turn off the heat.

    5.

    Heat oil or ghee in a deep frying pan over medium heat.

    6.

    Pour the batter into a squeeze bottle or a piping bag with a small hole.

    7.

    Squeeze the batter in a circular motion into the hot oil to form jalebi shapes.

    8.

    Fry until the jalebis are crisp and golden brown on both sides.

    9.

    Remove the jalebis from the oil and immediately dip them into the warm sugar syrup for a few seconds.

    10.

    Remove the jalebis from the syrup and place them on a plate to drain excess syrup.

    11.

    Serve the jalebis warm. Enjoy the sweet and crispy jalebis!

    • Mutton Biryani

    Mutton Biryani is a rich and flavorful rice dish made with tender mutton pieces, aromatic spices, and fragrant basmati rice. This classic dish is a staple in many South Asian cuisines and is often served at special occasions and gatherings.

    Ingredients

    • 500g mutton, cut into pieces
    • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes and drained
    • 2 onions, thinly sliced
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 1/2 cup yogurt
    • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 teaspoon chili powder
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves and fried onions for garnish (optional)

    Instructions

    1.

    Heat oil or ghee in a pressure cooker or heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat.

    2.

    Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.

    3.

    Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until fragrant.

    4.

    Add mutton pieces to the pot and cook until they turn brown.

    5.

    Add chopped tomatoes, chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 5-7 minutes.

    6.

    Add yogurt to the pot and mix well. Cook for another 5-7 minutes until the mutton is tender and the masala is well blended.

    7.

    Add soaked and drained rice to the pot, spreading it evenly over the mutton and masala.

    8.

    Pour water over the rice until it is completely submerged.

    9.

    Close the lid of the pressure cooker or pot and cook on medium heat for one whistle (if using a pressure cooker) or until the rice is cooked and water is absorbed.

    10.

    Once done, let the pressure release naturally before opening the lid.

    11.

    Fluff the biryani gently with a fork.

    12.

    Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and fried onions if desired.

    13.

    Serve hot with raita or salad. Enjoy your flavorful mutton biryani!

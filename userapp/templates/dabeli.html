    • Dabeli

    Dabeli is a popular street food from the Kutch region of Gujarat, India. It's a savory snack made by stuffing a soft bread roll (pav) with a spicy potato filling, pomegranate seeds, roasted peanuts, and a mix of chutneys.

    Ingredients

    • 6 burger buns or pav
    • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
    • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts, coarsely ground
    • 1/4 cup pomegranate seeds
    • 2 tablespoons dabeli masala
    • 2 tablespoons tamarind chutney
    • 2 tablespoons green chutney
    • 2 tablespoons oil
    • Salt to taste
    • Butter for toasting buns
    • Sev (fried gram flour vermicelli) for garnish
    • Coriander leaves for garnish

    Instructions

    1.

    Heat oil in a pan. Add mashed potatoes, dabeli masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.

    2.

    Cut the buns horizontally into halves. Apply butter on the inner sides and toast them on a griddle until golden brown.

    3.

    Spread green chutney on one side of the toasted buns and tamarind chutney on the other side.

    4.

    Place a portion of the potato mixture on the green chutney side of each bun.

    5.

    Sprinkle roasted peanuts and pomegranate seeds on top of the potato mixture.

    6.

    Close the buns with the tamarind chutney side and press gently.

    7.

    Heat a little oil on a griddle and roast the stuffed buns for a minute on each side.

    8.

    Remove from heat and garnish with sev and chopped coriander leaves.

    9.

    Serve hot with additional chutneys on the side. Enjoy your tasty dabeli!
